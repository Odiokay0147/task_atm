# ATM Transaction Analysis (2015–2017)

## Project Overview
This project analyzes ATM withdrawal patterns between January and June from 2015 to 2017.
The goal is to identify transaction trends, peak demand periods, ATM performance, and external factors that influence withdrawals such as working days and festival periods.

Understanding these patterns can help banks improve ATM cash management, network planning, and customer service efficiency.

# Dataset Description
The dataset consists of processed ATM transaction data covering January to June for the years 2015–2017.
### Dataset Columns
- ATM Name
- Date
- No Of Withdrawals
- No Of XYZ Card Withdrawals
- No Of Other Card Withdrawals
- Total Amount Withdrawn
- Amount Withdrawn XYZ Card
- Amount Withdrawn Other Card
- Weekday
- Festival Religion
- Working Day
- Holiday Sequence
- Processed Time
This information allows the analysis of time trends, ATM performance, customer behavior, and seasonal influences.

# Analysis & Insights

## 1. Monthly Transaction Trends
The monthly analysis captures the **total number of withdrawals between January and June for each year**.

- 2015: January recorded the highest withdrawals with 20,027 transactions.
- 2016: February had the highest withdrawals with 14,845 transactions.
- 2017: May recorded the highest withdrawals with 15,765 transactions, while January had the lowest.

Overall, the peak withdrawal period gradually shifted from the beginning of the year toward mid-year by 2017.

## 2. Weekly Transaction Patterns
Weekly analysis helps identify short-term fluctuations in ATM demand.

- 2015: Withdrawals increased early and peaked in Week 2 (5,556 withdrawals) before declining.
- 2016: Activity remained low during the first weeks and increased around Week 5, then gradually declined.
- 2017: Withdrawals started very low (265 withdrawals in Week 1) but increased gradually, peaking in Week 15 (4,232 withdrawals).

These trends suggest changes in ATM usage timing across the years.

## 3. Top Performing ATMs
ATM performance analysis identifies which locations handled the highest transaction volumes.

In 2015, the top performing ATM was Mount Road ATM, with 27,968 withdrawals, followed by Christ College ATM with 24,008 withdrawals.

In 2016, the ranking changed significantly. Christ College ATM became the top performer with 21,471 withdrawals, while Mount Road ATM dropped to the lowest position among the top ATMs with 4,732 withdrawals.

In 2017, Christ College ATM remained the top performing ATM with 26,922 withdrawals, while Mount Road ATM ranked near the bottom with 12,304 withdrawals.

This indicates that Christ College ATM maintained consistent high demand, while Mount Road ATM experienced a significant decline after 2015.

## 4. Card Usage Analysis
The analysis also compared withdrawals made using XYZ cards (the bank’s own cards) versus other bank cards.

In 2015, XYZ cards dominated ATM usage with 60,088 withdrawals, compared to 47,783 withdrawals from other cards.

In 2016, the trend remained similar, with XYZ cards (39,799) still exceeding other cards (35,994).

However, in 2017, the pattern reversed. Other cards recorded higher withdrawals (44,766) compared to XYZ cards (25,876).

This suggests that ATM usage by customers of other banks increased significantly by 2017, possibly indicating increased interbank ATM transactions.

## 5. Working Day vs Holiday Activity
The analysis examined whether ATM withdrawals were influenced by working days versus holidays.

In 2015, the average daily withdrawals were slightly higher on working days (121.27) compared to holidays (117.41).

In 2016, withdrawals were almost the same between working days (97.04) and holidays (97.91).

In 2017, withdrawals again became higher on working days (99.23) compared to holidays (91.03).

Overall, this suggests that ATM usage is slightly higher on working days but remains relatively stable across both day types.

## 6. Festival Impact
Festival periods also influenced withdrawal activity.

In 2015, the majority of withdrawal volume occurred during NH periods, totaling 450.68 million, significantly higher than other festival categories.

In 2016, NH remained dominant with 374.34 million withdrawals, followed by smaller contributions from other festival categories.

In 2017, NH still contributed the largest withdrawal volume (306.23 million), while N festivals showed a noticeable increase compared to previous years.

This indicates that festival periods can influence cash demand, particularly during major holidays.

## 7. Average Withdrawal Size (ATM Efficiency)
ATM efficiency was measured using average transaction size.

- 2015: Transaction size increased until March, slightly declined in April, then rose again in May.
- 2016: Similar pattern with a drop from April to May before rising in June.
- 2017: Transaction size increased steadily with only a minor decline from May to June.

This suggests customers generally **withdrew larger amounts as the months progressed**.

## 8. Withdrawal Growth Comparison (2015–2017)
A comparison of monthly withdrawals across the three years reveals a gradual decline in overall withdrawal volume.

For example, January withdrawals dropped from: 20,027 in 2015, 12,802 in 2016, and 5,276 in 2017. A similar pattern appears across most months.

This may indicate:
Increased use of digital banking channels
Reduced reliance on cash transactions
Possible changes in ATM network usage

# Key Findings

1. ATM withdrawals declined steadily from 2015 to 2017
2. Withdrawal peaks shifted from early-year to mid-year
3. Christ College ATM consistently performed as the top ATM
4. Other bank card usage increased significantly by 2017
5. ATM usage is slightly higher on working days
6. Festival periods can influence higher withdrawal volumes
7. Average withdrawal amounts increase gradually during the year
